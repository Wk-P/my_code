P3 PPO — Unconstrained Baseline

GT — Greater (N > M) Vanilla PPO

ECU count exceeds service count; capacity constraints are loose and placement is more flexible.

Problem Definition

Decision variable: assignment matrix $x_{ij} \in \{0,1\}$, where $x_{ij}=1$ means service $i$ is assigned to ECU $j$.

Scale: $N$ ECUs, $M$ services, N > M (N/M > 1)

$$ \text{AR} = \frac{\sum_{j=1}^{N} \sum_{i=1}^{M} x_{ij} \cdot \frac{d_i}{C_j}} {|\{j :\exists\, i,\, x_{ij}=1\}|} $$

AR = Average Resource Utilisation; objective is to maximise AR while satisfying capacity and conflict constraints.

Constraints

Capacity Constraint

$$\sum_{i=1}^{M} x_{ij} \cdot d_i \leq C_j \quad \forall j \in [N]$$

Conflict Constraint

$$\forall \mathcal{F}_k \in \mathcal{F},\ \forall j :\quad \sum_{i \in \mathcal{F}_k} x_{ij} \leq 1$$

$\mathcal{F} = \{\mathcal{F}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{F}_K\}$ is the family of conflict sets; services within the same set cannot share an ECU.

Markov Decision Process (MDP)

ElementDefinition
State $s_t$Observation vector; see Observation Space below
Action $a_t$Choose ECU index $a_t \in \{0,\ldots,N-1\}$
TransitionDeterministic: assign service $t$ to ECU $a_t$, update remaining capacity
Episode Length$T = M$ steps (one service placed per step)
Discount Factor$\gamma = 0.99$

Observation Space

State vector $s_t \in \mathbb{R}^{\text{dim}}$ concatenated in order:

DimensionDescriptionFormula
[0]Current service demand (normalised)$d_t / C_{\max}$
[1]Cumulative AR so far$AR_t$
[2]Total remaining capacity (normalised)$\sum_j \text{rem}_j^t / \sum_j C_j$
[3]Total remaining service demand (normalised)$\sum_{i \geq t} d_i / \sum_j C_j$
[4]Fraction of ECUs with sufficient remaining capacity$|\{j : \text{rem}_j^t \geq d_t\}| / N$
[5]Fraction of services yet to be placed$(M - t) / M$
[6:6+N]Initial capacity ratio per ECU$C_j / C_{\max}$
[6+N:6+2N]Remaining capacity per ECU (can be negative)$\text{clip}(\text{rem}_j^t, -C_{\max}, C_{\max}) / C_{\max}$
[6+2N:6+3N]Conflict flag per ECU$\mathbf{1}[\text{conflict}(j, t)]$
[6+3N:6+4N]Valid-capacity flag per ECU$\mathbf{1}[\text{rem}_j^t \geq d_t]$
Middle M dimsValid ECU count per future service$\text{valid\_ecus}_i / N$
Last M dimsRemaining service demand list$d_i / C_{\max}$ for $i \geq t$

Reward Function

Step Reward

$$ r_t = \underbrace{\frac{ru_t}{K^t}}_{\text{step reward}} + \text{penalties}_t + b_T \cdot \mathbf{1}[t = M] $$

where:

$$ ru_t = \frac{d_t}{C_{a_t}},\quad K^t = |\{j : \text{ECU}\,j\,\text{in use}\}| $$

Geometric Interpretation of Step Reward

$$ \frac{ru_t}{K^t} = \begin{cases} \Delta AR_t & \text{if placed on existing ECU (}K^t = K^{t-1}\text{）} \\ \dfrac{ru_t}{K^{t-1}+1} > 0 & \text{if new ECU activated (}K^t = K^{t-1}+1\text{）} \end{cases} $$

When placed on an existing ECU, $ru_t/K^t = \Delta AR_t > 0$. When a new ECU is activated the reward stays positive — necessary expansion is not penalised, but packing is incentivised: for the same $ru_t$, smaller $K^t$ yields a higher reward.

Terminal Bonus

$$b_T = AR_T \quad (\text{unconditional, violations not penalised})$$

Penalties: none (penalties = 0); all violations are recorded but not penalised.

Algorithm: Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO)

Objective Function

$$ \mathcal{L}^{\text{CLIP}}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_t\Big[ \min\Big(\rho_t(\theta)\hat{A}_t,\; \text{clip}(\rho_t(\theta), 1-\varepsilon, 1+\varepsilon)\hat{A}_t \Big)\Big] $$

where the probability ratio $\rho_t(\theta) = \dfrac{\pi_\theta(a_t|s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}(a_t|s_t)}$ and clipping parameter $\varepsilon = 0.2$.

Generalised Advantage Estimation (GAE)

$$ \hat{A}_t = \sum_{l=0}^{T-t} (\gamma\lambda)^l \delta_{t+l},\quad \delta_t = r_t + \gamma V_\phi(s_{t+1}) - V_\phi(s_t) $$

Constraint Handling

P3 is the unconstrained baseline: no action mask and no violation penalty.

Violation consequence: episode continues; only counts are recorded in info.

Expected behaviour: the agent learns to stack all services onto a single ECU (AR > 1) to maximise $\sum ru_t / K^t$ (minimise denominator). This is P3's design intent — a control showing the failure mode of unconstrained RL.
